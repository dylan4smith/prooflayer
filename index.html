What ProofLayer Catches

The errors you can't spot at a glance

Range boundary errors

SUM covers rows 3–12 when data runs to row 13. The most common AI mistake — nearly invisible.

VLOOKUP column mismatches

Lookup pulls col 4 but intended data is col 5. Cross-referenced against header names.

Phantom values

Hardcoded numbers that don't exist in the raw data. Pure hallucinations — numbers AI invented.

Intent vs. behavior

"Total Revenue" that sums costs. AI checks whether formula logic matches its label.

Consistency drift

Row 15 uses different logic than rows 1–14. AI tools introduce subtle mid-column inconsistencies.

Cross-sheet breaks

References between sheets that silently drop or transform data. Full chain traced raw → output.

Questions

FAQ

Does ProofLayer use AI to verify the math?

No — the core engine is 100% deterministic. It re-executes every formula using a math engine, not an LLM. AI is only used for semantic intent analysis — checking whether a formula labeled "Total Revenue" actually sums revenue.

What AI tools does it work with?

Any. ProofLayer works on any .xlsx or .csv regardless of how it was created — Claude Cowork, ChatGPT, Copilot, a colleague, or yourself.

Is my data secure?

Files are processed in encrypted, isolated environments and deleted after verification. We never store your spreadsheet data — only the verification report metadata. Waitlist emails are collected via encrypted HTTPS and stored securely.

How is this different from Excel's formula auditing?

Excel shows WHERE a formula points. ProofLayer tells you WHETHER the formula is CORRECT by independently re-computing against raw data and checking intent vs. behavior.
